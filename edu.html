ANIMATOR EDUCATION

You don’t need a degree to become an animator, although the technical training you receive at school can help. There are plenty of resources available online to learn animation software on your own. While many animators and artists are self-taught,formal education can give you the head start you need to speed up your career and get your work in front of the right people. In addition to online tutorials and certifications for common animation apps, you can take classes taught by professionals. The hands-on feedback you receive can really help you understand your strengths and opportunities. Starting in high school, students can take art and computer science classes to build a foundation. Participating in art clubs or online communities can also provide valuable experience. Associate Degree: Typically takes about two years. This can be a good stepping stone but may limit advanced career opportunities. Bachelor’s Degree: Usually takes four years. This is the most common pathway and provides comprehensive training in animation principles and techniques. Master’s Degree: This takes an additional two to three years after the bachelor’s degree. This is ideal for those looking to specialize or pursue academic careers.
