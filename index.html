ABOUT ANIMATOR

An animator is a digital artist who creates images that move or simulate movement in sequence. Animators can work in many fields, such as television, film, video games, and advertising. Animators do more than create cartoons or 3D animated movies like Toy Story, however. They can also work as effects artists to create the dynamic elements you see in live-action films, TV shows and commercials, and web graphics. Animators often specialize in different aspects of animation production, such as character design or special effects, and their job duties differ depending on their specialties. Whatever their specialties, animators typically use one of the three primary animation techniques: 2D, 3D, or stop-motion.

Animator types

  • Animator
  • 3D creative designer
  • Video game designer
  • 3D artist
  • Art director
  • 3D animator
  • Special effects artist and animator or visual effects (VFX) artist

MORE ABOUT 2D & 3D

2D animation is the art of creating movement in a two-dimensional space. 2D animators must have strong drawing abilities and a great sense of motion, timing, and sequencing as they animate characters and backgrounds. 3D animation (a.k.a. computer animation) It involves creating images in three-dimensional environments that are then animated to look like moving objects. 3D animators need to understand anatomy, perspective, light, and shadow.
